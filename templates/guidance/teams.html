How to Use Microsoft Teams from Start to Finish

Before your first DIICSU class
  1. Install Teams (desktop or mobile) or use the web version.
  2. Sign in with your school Microsoft account (or the account provided by DIICSU).
  3. Join the class Team (invite link or code) and pin it.
  4. Find the class chat and your project-group chat (if applicable).
  5. Do a quick audio test: choose the right mic/speaker.
When it's time to join a meeting
  1. Open the meeting from Calendar or the teacher's link.
  2. Check camera and microphone before you click Join now.
  3. If you enter the lobby, wait until the teacher admits you.
  4. Use Chat for questions, and Raise hand if needed.
  5. After class, check the meeting chat and Files for materials.
Common mistakes (avoid these)
  • Joining with the wrong account (personal vs school) and not seeing the class Team.
  • Clicking Join too early without checking mic/speaker, then you can't hear the teacher.
  • Posting homework questions in the wrong place (class channel vs private chat).

Getting Started with Teams

To begin using Teams, follow these steps:

  1. Download and install Microsoft Teams on your device, or use the web version at teams.microsoft.com.
  2. Sign in with your Microsoft account (for DIICSU: use your school account if provided).
  3. In a supported browser (Edge/Chrome), you can use Teams without downloading.

Tip: click your profile picture to set your status (Available, Busy, Do Not Disturb).

Creating and Managing Teams

A Team is a workspace for a group such as a department, project group, or class.

To create a new team:

  1. Click the Teams tab.
  2. Select Join or create a team.
  3. Click Create team, then choose to start from scratch or from an existing group.
  4. Name your team, add members, and set privacy options.

You can also join existing teams with a code or an invite link.

Using Private and Group Chats

The Chat feature allows direct messaging between individuals or small groups. For DIICSU, this is where you'll most often communicate with your project group.

To start a chat:

  1. Click the Chat tab.
  2. Select the New chat icon.
  3. Enter names, then begin messaging.

You can also start voice/video calls, share files, and schedule meetings within any chat. Use @mentions to get someone's attention.

Scheduling and Joining Meetings

The built-in Calendar helps you organize and join meetings easily. For DIICSU students, your main job is to join the teacher's meeting link correctly and be ready to speak.

How to join a meeting (DIICSU checklist)
  1. Open the meeting link from your teacher or open Calendar → select the meeting → Join.
  2. On the pre-join screen, confirm:
    • Correct speaker (you can hear)
    • Correct microphone (it moves when you speak)
    • Camera on/off based on teacher rules
  3. Set your name clearly (e.g., "DIICSU_YourName" if required).
  4. Click Join now. If you enter the lobby, wait to be admitted.
  5. If you can't hear anything: open More (...) > Settings > Device settings and re-select speaker/mic.

If you need to create your own meeting: Calendar → New meeting → fill details → Save.

In meetings, you can
  • Share your screen
  • Use chat and reactions
  • Enable live captions
  • Record the meeting (if permitted)

Sharing Files and Collaborating on Documents

Microsoft Teams makes it easy to upload and work on files with your team.

  • Use the Attach icon in any chat or channel to upload files.
  • Files shared in chats are stored in OneDrive.
  • Files shared in channels go to SharePoint.
  • You can collaborate in real time on Word, Excel, and PowerPoint without leaving Teams.

Managing Presence and Notifications

You can control how and when you appear available to others.

  • Click your profile image to set your status (Available, Busy, Do Not Disturb, etc.).
  • Add a custom status message if needed.
  • Go to Settings > Notifications to control alerts for chats, mentions, and meetings.
  • Presence indicators help others know your availability; read receipts show message delivery.
